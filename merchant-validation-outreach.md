# Merchant Validation — Outreach Kit

*Goal: 5 real conversations with apparel/footwear store owners to confirm (or kill) the size-level forecasting wedge before you write code. This is the only validation that actually matters.*

**Your green light:** 3 of 5 describe the size/reorder pain *unprompted* AND say they'd try or pay for a fix.
**Your red light:** they shrug — "spreadsheets are fine." Believe them, pivot to bundles/BOM.

---

## Where to find them

- **r/shopify, r/smallbusiness, r/ecommerce** — search "inventory," "reorder," "sizes," "dead stock." Comment on relevant threads, then DM.
- **r/AppHookup / Shopify Discord / Indie Hackers** — founders who talk shop.
- **Instagram / TikTok** — small clothing brands are everywhere. DM the ones with ~5k–100k followers (big enough to have reorder pain, small enough to read their own DMs).
- **Shopify Community forums** — reply to inventory/forecasting threads.
- **Your own network** — anyone who runs or works at a clothing brand. Warm intros convert best.
- **LinkedIn** — search "founder" + "apparel" / "clothing brand," filter small companies.

Aim to contact ~30–40 to land 5 real conversations. Most won't reply. That's normal.

---

## The rules that make this work

1. **Don't pitch. Don't mention your app.** The moment they smell a sales pitch, they tell you what's polite, not what's true.
2. **Ask about their past behavior, not future intentions.** "What did you do last reorder?" beats "Would you use a tool that…?" People lie about the future, not the past.
3. **Shut up and listen.** Your job is to hear the pain in their words, or hear that it isn't there.
4. **Ask how they solve it now and what it costs them.** No current workaround = no real pain. A painful workaround = opportunity.

## Copy-paste: Reddit / forum DM

> Hey — I saw you run [brand]. Not selling anything, I'm researching how clothing brands handle inventory. Quick one if you have 2 min: when it's time to reorder, how do you decide how much of each *size* to buy? Spreadsheet, gut, an app? Curious what actually works for you.

## The 5 questions (work them into the conversation naturally)

1. Walk me through your **last reorder** — how did you decide quantities per size?
2. What do you use today — spreadsheet, gut, an app? (Which one?)
3. How often do you **run out of popular sizes** or get **stuck with dead stock** in the wrong ones?
4. Roughly what does that cost you — in lost sales or markdowns?
5. If something nailed the size-level forecast for you, would you pay for it? What's "too expensive"?

---

## How to read the answers (be honest with yourself)

| Signal | What it means |
|---|---|
| They describe the size-curve pain *before you mention it* | Strong green — real pain |
| They already pay for a tool but complain about it | Strong green — proven budget + opening |
| They use a painful spreadsheet every reorder | Green — pain + DIY = opportunity |
| "It's fine, I just eyeball it" / small catalog | Red — no real pain here |
| Polite interest but no current workaround | Red — that's politeness, not demand |

Tally honestly. Don't talk yourself into a green that isn't there — that's the expensive mistake this whole exercise exists to prevent.

---

## After the 5 conversations

- **Green:** you'll have their exact words — use them as your app's landing-page copy and your v1 feature priorities. Then build. Ask the warmest 1–2 if they'd be a free beta tester (your first reviews).
- **Red:** you just saved yourself weeks of building the wrong thing. Bring the notes back and we'll re-aim at bundles or BOM with the same rigor.
